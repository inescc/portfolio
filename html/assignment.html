Connecting students from different fields of knowledge through skill exchange

Challenge

Academic success is difficult to achieve when lacking certain skills (e.g. paper writing, programming, statistics, design).

Unfortunately, most students don't have resources to hire tutors or getting professional help.

One thing students have though, is spare time.

Why not creating a network to allow them to help each other out? Why not use time as currency?

We propose assignment.com, a social web for students to exchange skills for time.

What is assignment.com?

It is a network connecting students with different skill-sets. It allows undergraduates to receive/provide help to others. It is also a way for graduates to build or grow their own portfolio. Lastly, it facilitates meeting people through the creation of teams.

Who designed assignment.com?

I designed assignment.com with some colleagues from the Human Centred Multimedia master. Being ourselves students, it was easier to identify the needs of our target audience.

Research

Target audience: Undergraduates and graduates looking for their first job.

Goal: To design a social network aiming at connecting students from different fields of knowledge by exchanging their skills

Research question: What are the main requirements of a such an application? How can it engage participants without involving money?

Evaluation

We followed an iterative design process. Paper prototypes and wireframes were evaluated by 5 volunteer students through usability tests and interviews. The final working prototype could not be tested due to technical constraints--it was not fully operating.

1. Wireframes usability test

Goal: To evaluate the information flow and architecture.

Tasks:

  1. Edit your profile to add a new skill of your choice
  2. Look for an Expert (level) in Python (skill) who is available on Wednesdays

2. Interview

Participants were provided with a post-study questionnaire including the following questions:

  1. Did you have any difficulties using assignment.com? If so, please describe them.
  2. How easy/difficult was to use assignment.com? (1 - very difficult; 5 - very easy)?
  3. How willing would you be to use assignment.com? (1 - very unwilling; 5 - very willing)?
